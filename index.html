SpartaHack is Michigan State University’s official hackathon. For one weekend, 300 students from a wide range of disciplines and skill levels will team up to build creative, innovative technological projects from scratch.

When is SpartaHack?

Spartahack will take place from Friday, March 27th until Sunday, March 29th. You’ll have 36 hours to design, code, and build.

Do I need to know how to code?

Not at all! We welcome a wide range of skill levels, from newcomer to the experienced programmer. Hackathons are a great place to learn a new skill!

Who can participate?

Because our event is sanctioned by Major League Hacking, participation is limited to students or those who have left school within 2014-2015 school year. As of right now, we aren’t accepting participants under 18 years of age, but we’ll keep you updated if that changes!

How do I register?

We love your eagerness, but our registration is not quite ready to be released to the world. However, feel free to join our mailing list to get notified when you can sign yourself up.

Are there registration costs?

SpartaHack, just like America, is free! We’ll feed you, clothe you (in a single t-shirt - so bring pants) and deck you out in swag.

How many students can be on a team?

Teams can consist of 1 - 4 students. If you don’t have a team: don’t worry! We’ll have team formation options on-site.

Are there prizes?

Absolutely! Prizes are awarded for many categories, from the Grand Prize, to the Best Use of API. There is even a First-Time Hacker Prize!

Can I start the project before the weekend?

Unfortunately not. You are welcome to come with ideas beforehand, but all your work must be done on site (don’t worry, you’ll have 36 hours).

Will you reimburse my travel?

We are hoping to have an answer to this soon. Check back to our website for updates.

What should I bring?

You will need a laptop, a student ID/picture ID, toiletries, and, of course, your enthusiasm for the weekend.

Where will I be staying?

Our event will be held in Wells Hall on Michigan State University’s campus. For 36 hours….THIS will be your home.

How is the hackathon judged?

Judging will be done by experienced professionals in the industry. More details to be released closer to the event!

Who is organizing this event?

SpartaHack is organized by a group of Michigan State University students dedicated to inspiring more students to get excited about technical innovation.

Where should I direct further questions?

Shoot us an email at hello@spartahack.com if you have any questions or concerns.
